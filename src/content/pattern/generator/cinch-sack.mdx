  # Assembly Instructions

  <DividedSection header="Materials">
    <Image
      format="webp"
      width={225}
      aspectRatio="1/1"
      src={import("@/images/cinchsack/materials.jpg")}
      alt="stuff sack materials"
      class="float-right rounded-lg"

    See our [Fabrics Guide](article/fabrics) for technical fabrics, recommendations, and trusted vendors.
    Lighter weight materials such as 1.9oz ripstop nylon work best for drawstring channels.

    - This project uses two fabric panels: Body panel & Cord Channel.
    - One drawstring cut to **6 inches (15 cm) longer than Body Panel Length (<span class="patternLength"/>)**
    - One cord lock compatible with the drawstring.
    - One 4 inch (10 cm) piece of webbing or ribbon.


  </DividedSection>

  ---

  <DividedSection header="Body Panel">
    <Image
      format="webp"
      width={225}
      aspectRatio="1/1"
      src={import("@/images/cinchsack/cinch2.jpg")}
      alt="Body panel pattern"
      class="float-right "
    />

    1. Measure and cut one fabric body panel. **Length: <span class="patternLength"/>** and **Height:
    <span class="patternHeight"/>**.
    2. Mark the mid point of the panel along top and bottom edges.
    3. Along the top, mark the **<span class="sA"/>** seam allowance from the left and right side.

    In the illustrations, the fabric's wrong side is light blue.


  </DividedSection>

  ---

  <DividedSection header="Drawstring Channel">
    <Image
      format="webp"
      width={225}
      aspectRatio="1/1"
      src={import("@/images/cinchsack/cinch3.jpg")}
      alt="cord channel pattern"
      class="float-right "
    />

    1. Measure and cut one cord channel panel. **Length: <span class="channelLength"/>** and **Height: <span
    class="channelHeight"/>**.
    1. Fold and stitch a **<span class="sA"/>** single fold hem along one end of Cord Channel.
    1. Repeat **<span class="sA"/>** single fold hem on opposite side.
    1. Fold the cord channel right sides out (RSO). Align top and bottom edge. Baste stitch inside **<span class="sA"/>**
    seam allowance.
    1. Baste stitch a webbing pull loop at the mid point of Cord Channel.

  </DividedSection>

  ---

  <DividedSection header="Attach Cord Channel">
    <Image
      format="webp"
      width={225}
      aspectRatio="1/1"
      src={import("@/images/cinchsack/cinch4.jpg")}
      alt="Sew channel to body"
      class="float-right"
    />

    1. Center align Cord Channel to top edge of Body Panel, right sides together (RST).
    1. Note: the Cord Channel should be inset **<span class="sA"/>** from the sides of Body Panel.
    1. Confirm webbing loop is sandwiched between Body and Cord Channel, then sew Cord Channel to Body Panel.
    1. Depending on how quickly your fabric frays, you may consider overcasting the raw edge with a zigzag stitch.
  </DividedSection>

  ---

  <DividedSection header="Body Seams">
    <Image
      format="webp"
      width={224}
      aspectRatio="1/1"
      src={import("@/images/cinchsack/cinch5.jpg")}
      alt="Stitch side and bottom"
      class="float-right"
    />

    1. Flip the Cord Channel up and finger press the seam down towards the Body Panel.
    1. Along top edge of Body Panel just below Cord Channel, topstitch through Body Panel and seam allowances.
    1. Fold the Body Panel in half, right sides together (RST).
    1. Starting at the Cord Channel openings, stitch the Body Panel sides together.
    1. Center align the side seam. Finger press the seam to one side.
    1. Stitch the bottom closed.

  </DividedSection>

  ---

  <DividedSection header="Squaring the Bottom">
    <Image
      format="webp"
      width={225}
      aspectRatio="1/1"
      src={import("@/images/cinchsack/cinch6.jpg")}
      alt="Sew squared corners"
      class="float-right"
    />

    1. Blow a little air into the stuff sack and press the sides in to form sharp 90 degree corners.
    1. With the bottom seam finger pressed flat and straight, draw a mark from the left side:
      - 1/2 Bottom Width = **<span class="halfBottomWidth"/>**
      - Bottom Length = **<span class="bottomLength"/>**
      - 1/2 Bottom Width **<span class="halfBottomWidth"/>** should remain.
    1. At first mark, draw a **<span class="bottomWidth"/>** line (Bottom Width) perpendicular and
    centered on bottom seam.
    1. Repeat same on vertical line at other mark.
    1. The bottom should look like the image ->
    1. Stitch along the vertical lines forming the rectangular bottom.
    1. Before trimming off the corners, flip right side out (RSO) to check for overall symmetry and desired size.
    1. Trim the corners off about 1/2" (1 cm) from the seam. Overcast or edge bind to prevent fraying.

  </DividedSection>

  ---

  <DividedSection header="Finishing Up">
    <Image
      format="webp"
      width={225}
      aspectRatio="1/1"
      src={import("@/images/cinchsack/sample.jpg")}
      alt="sample stuff sack"
      class="float-right rounded-xl"
    />

    1. Flip the cinch sack right side out (RSO).
    1. Where Cord Channel openings meet the Body Panel, add a small reinforcement stitch.
    1. With a safety pin or paracord fid, thread the drawstring through the Cord Channel.
    1. Add the cord lock and tie the free ends of the drawstring together.
