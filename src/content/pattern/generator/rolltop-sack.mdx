  # Assembly Instructions

  <DividedSection header="Materials">
    See our <a href="article/fabrics">Fabrics Guide</a> for technical fabrics, recommendations, and trusted vendors.

      Medium-weight fabric such as HyperD 300 or 200d Ripstop Nylon work great for water resistant gear storage.

      - This project only uses one fabric panel: Body panel.
      - One **<span class="webbingLength">long</span>** piece of 3/4" (20mm) webbing.
      - One side release buckle compatible with the webbing.
      - One 4 inch (10 cm) piece of narrow webbing or ribbon.

  </DividedSection>

  ---

  <DividedSection header="Body Panel">
    <Image
      format="webp"

    1. Measure and cut one fabric Body Panel. **Length: <span class="patternLength"/>** and **Height: <span class="patternHeight"/>**.
    1. On wrong side of fabric, mark the midpoint of the panel along the bottom edge.
    1. Parallel the top edge, mark the **<span class="hem"/>** hem line from left to right side.

    In the illustrations, the fabric's wrong side is light blue.

  </DividedSection>

  ---

  <DividedSection header="Roll-Top Collar">
    <Image
      format="webp"
      width={225}
      aspectRatio="1/1"
      src={import("@/images/rolltopsack/rt3.jpg")}
      alt="Roll-top collar prep"
      class="float-right rounded-lg"
    />


    1. Double fold a hem over the **<span class="hem"/>** hem line and sew.
    1. On the right side of Body Panel fabric, mark the midpoint of the hem.
    1. Center align webbing on top of hem. Clip in place at midpoint.
    1. Arrange buckle hardware and fold under webbing. Clip in place.
    1. Tip: Double check your hardware orientation is right side out (RSO)!
    1. Ensure the length of webbing from folded end-to-end is slightly longer than **<span class="halfTop"/>**.
    1. Stitch webbing along hem. Parallel stitch lines along top and bottom edges of webbing suffice.
    1. Reinforce buckle hardware with bar tack or box stitch. Repeat opposite side.

  </DividedSection>

  ---

  <DividedSection header="Body Seams">
    <Image
      format="webp"
      width={225}
      aspectRatio="1/1"
      src={import("@/images/rolltopsack/rt4.jpg")}
      alt="Stitch side and bottom"
      class="float-right rounded-lg"
    />


    1. Fold the Body Panel in half, right sides out (RSO), aligning left and right edges.
    1. Stitch the Body Panel sides together for a french seam. RSO, sew with less than half of the **<span class="sA"/>** seam allowance.
    1. Flip right sides together (RST) and complete the french seam. RST, sew using the remainder of the **<span class="sA"/>** seam allowance.
    1. Tip: If you machine struggles to sew thru two double fold hems, trim away some of the inner most fabric within the hem.
    1. Flip the bag right side out (RSO) and center align the side seam.
    1. Stitch the Body Panel bottom together for a french seam. RSO, sew with less than half of the **<span class="sA"/>** seam allowance.
    1. Flip right sides together (RST) and center align a small loop of webbing, sandwiched inside Body Panel. Clip in place.
    1. Complete the french seam. RST, sew using the remainder of the **<span class="sA"/>** seam allowance.
    1. Reinforce the webbing loop with a few back stitches.

  </DividedSection>

  ---

  <DividedSection header="Squaring the Bottom">
    <Image
      format="webp"
      width={225}
      aspectRatio="1/1"
      src={import("@/images/rolltopsack/rt5.jpg")}
      alt="Sew squared corners"
      class="float-right rounded-lg"

    1. Blow a little air into the stuff sack and press the sides in to form sharp 90 degree corners.
    1. With the bottom seam finger pressed flat and straight, draw a mark from the left
      - 1/2 Bottom Width = **<span class="halfBottomWidth"/>**
      - Bottom Length = **<span class="bottomLength"/>**
      - 1/2 Bottom Width **<span class="halfBottomWidth"/>** should remain.
    1. At first mark, draw a **<span class="bottomWidth"/>** line (Bottom Width) perpendicular and centered on bottom seam.
    1. Repeat same on vertical line at other mark.
    1. The bottom should look like the image ->
    1. Stitch along the vertical lines forming the rectangular bottom.
    1. Before trimming off the corners, flip right side out (RSO) to check for overall symmetry and desired size.
    1. Trim the corners off about 1/2" (1 cm) from the seam. If your fabric frays, overcast or edge bind the raw edges.


  </DividedSection>

    1. Flip the roll-top sack right side out (RSO).
    2. Roll the top edge down three or four times using the webbing as a guide. Secure the quick release buckle.
