Zipper Theory

Zipper Choices

Zippers come in many sizes and styles for various apparel and bag making applications. For zipper pouchs and backpacks, plastic coil zippers offer a good balance of durability and cost. Compared to "Vislon" molded tooth zippers, coil zippers work well along curves. Plastic teeth are less likely to scratch your belongings and won't corrode like metal teeth.
Additionally, coil zippers are easily sourced at local crafts stores or online fabric stores in fixed lenghts with little variation in cost. Most specialty stores offer coil zipper tape off a spool since plastic toothed zippers can be cut with scissors or rotary tool. These stores sell sliders of various styles that match the tape of choice. Note, sliders and zipper tape are matches so be sure to select compatible items.
Water resistant zippers are now commonplace, however are significantly more expensive and the polyurethane (PU) coating is subject to UV damage. PU coated zippers are harder to find in small tooth sizes (less than #5) and require more force to open and close than the standard options.

Zipper Sizes

For ultralight and backpacking zip pouches, size #3 or #5 coil zippers are recommended.
