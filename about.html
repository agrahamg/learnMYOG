Where to have patterns printed on large format paper?
Most print shops will have 36" wide paper (NA) or A0 (UK/EU). My preferred online print shop is Pattern Printing Company. They offer color prints on 16# paper in low quantities, shipped folded. They are fast and their customer service has been great. Another popular option is PDF Plotting.

Which needles and thread to get started?
Gutermann Sew-All polyester thread. Once you get more serious, buy the same thread in larger quantities under the name Gutermann Mara 100. Wawak.com will email you a color conversion reference table if requested. For slightly thicker and stronger thread, Mara 70 is great to have on hand. For needles, a small pack of Universal needles in the #11-16 size range will get you started. Microtex/Sharp #12-14 is best for ultralight fabrics less than 30 denier.
What's the best sewing machine for getting started?
Machines are commonly available to borrow. Look for a maker space. Check out your local library system for tool loans. If purchasing a new or used machine look for the following:
  • Mechanical straight stitch machine with reverse lever
  • Atleast 1/4" presser foot height, often called two-stage lifter
  • 1-4mm+ minimum stitch length and 5-6mm stitch width
  • Utility stitches including zigzag, overlock/overcast, and stretch
  • Automatic button hole (with accessory foot) is a great addition
  • Walking foot attachment is helpful for sewing slippery layers
  • Needle size compatibility should range #11/75-16/100+
  • Quality customer support and local service center
While I have limited to no experience with these specific machines, they meet the desired attributes listed.

Serger yay or nay?
I use my serger on almost every project. If you're making apparel or regularly sewing knits, yes, find a used serger. But its a second machine, not a replacement for a lockstitch machine.

What software do you use for patterning?
I create digital sewing patterns using Inkscape, a free and open source tool primarily for illustrators. Outside of Inkscape, I use homegrown python scripts to automate the boring repetitive tasks. If that's up your alley, learn more at Github.
